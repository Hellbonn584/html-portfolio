The best movies I have ever seen

Top 3

Interstellar (2014)

Christopher Nolan masterfully combines stunning visuals, an emotional storyline, and Hans Zimmer’s mesmerizing score to create an unforgettable cinematic experience. The film’s thought-provoking themes about love, sacrifice, and survival leave a lasting impression, making it one of the most profound space exploration movies of all time.

Fight Club (1999)

Directed by David Fincher and based on Chuck Palahniuk’s novel, the film delivers a gripping narrative, intense performances (especially by Edward Norton and Brad Pitt), and a shocking twist that leaves viewers questioning reality. Its raw energy and philosophical undertones make it a cult classic that remains relevant today.

Green Book (2018)

Directed by Peter Farrelly, the film follows the unlikely friendship between an African-American pianist, Dr. Don Shirley (Mahershala Ali), and his Italian-American driver, Tony Lip (Viggo Mortensen), during a racially tense journey through the 1960s American South. With brilliant performances, humor, and emotional depth, "Green Book" delivers a powerful message about prejudice, humanity, and understanding.
